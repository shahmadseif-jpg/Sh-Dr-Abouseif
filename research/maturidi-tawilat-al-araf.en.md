## About the Thesis

A scholarly critical edition (taḥqīq) and study of the tafsīr of Imām Abū Manṣūr al-Māturīdī (d. 333 AH), entitled **Taʾwīlāt Ahl al-Sunna**, within the compass of **Sūrat al-Aʿrāf**. In it the researcher undertakes to fix and critically establish the text of the commentary against its manuscript originals, to collate its several copies, and to provide takhrīj for its verses, its ḥadīths, and the statements of the exegetes (ahl al-taʾwīl), while documenting the manuscript variants (furūq al-nusakh) in the apparatus, after the manner of the critical editors.

The critical edition is preceded by an **introductory study** in three chapters: the first treats the age of Imām al-Māturīdī and his life; the second, his contributions to tafsīr; and the third, his method in exegesis — in his interpretation of the verses of creed (āyāt al-ʿaqīda), the verses of legal rulings (āyāt al-aḥkām), and his stance toward the rules of the Arabic language — together with a description of the manuscript copies and the methodology of the edition, followed by a conclusion presenting its principal findings.

Among the aims of the thesis are: to bring to light the standing of al-Māturīdī as an exegete; to disclose his method of "interpretation" (taʾwīl) upon the foundations of the people of the Sunna; and to render service to a precious exegetical text by issuing it in a sound, carefully established critical edition within the compass of Sūrat al-Aʿrāf.

## Contents of the Introductory Study

- **The Introduction**, the research methodology, and the description of the manuscript copies.
- **The Prefatory Study** (three chapters):
  - Chapter One: The age of Imām al-Māturīdī and his life (the political life of the era, his biography, and his scholarly formation).
  - Chapter Two: His contributions to tafsīr.
  - Chapter Three: His method in exegesis — in the verses of creed, the verses of legal rulings, and in relation to the rules of the Arabic language.
- **The Critical Edition:** the text of Taʾwīlāt Ahl al-Sunna for Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, critically established, with the manuscript variants and the takhrīj set out in the footnotes at the foot of each page.
- **The Conclusion:** the principal findings and recommendations.
